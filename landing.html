FAQ

It really doesn't
touch my bank account?

Where does my data go?
By default — nowhere. Your subscription list lives in your browser's localStorage on this device. No account, no analytics, no third-party trackers. If you opt into Pro Cloud Sync, the data is end-to-end encrypted on your device (AES-GCM 256, key derived from your master password) and stored in your own Google Drive's hidden app folder. We literally cannot read it. Full breakdown in our Privacy Policy.
How does the bank-statement import work?
Download a CSV from Revolut, Wise, N26, PayPal, Chase, or Amex (all banks let you do this in 2 clicks), drop it into Leakd. The parser detects recurring charges by amount + interval, in 28 languages of column headers. The whole thing runs in your browser — no upload, no bank login, no third-party aggregator.
What's the Business Expense Report?
Mark any subscription as a business expense, and the Pro report bundles them into a structured PDF + CSV: vendor links, billing cycles, monthly/yearly totals, your share when split with family. It helps you stay organized — but it's for informational purposes only and does not constitute tax, legal, or accounting advice. Always verify figures with a qualified professional.
What if I clear my browser cache?
On Free, the data is gone — which is why we have one-click backup to JSON and a calendar (.ics) export under Settings. On Pro, multi-device sync via your Google Drive pulls it back automatically when you sign in.
Where can I buy the Pro plan?
Pro is sold via Google Play Billing — $5/month or $40/year. Your invoice lives in your Google Play account. Refunds within 48 hours are processed automatically by Google; later requests via hello@leakd.app. Full details in our Refund Policy.
Does it work offline?
Yes. After the first load it's a Progressive Web App — installable to your home screen, fully usable offline. Currency rates refresh once a day when online; everything else is local.
What if you shut down the service?
Free Leakd runs in your browser — even if our domain went away tomorrow, the installed PWA would keep working and you could export your data to CSV/JSON. Pro features that touch the network (Cloud Sync, Email Reminders) would stop, but your data is yours either way.
Why "Leakd"?
Money rarely disappears in big chunks — it leaks: small recurring amounts that compound into a serious yearly bill. The name reminds you why you opened the app.
