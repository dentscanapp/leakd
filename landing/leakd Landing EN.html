Our principles

Four principles we design the product around.

Most finance apps grow by asking for more access. We aim for the opposite — full details are in our Security page and Privacy Policy.

01

Local storage by default.

Your subscription list is stored in your browser's localStorage. We don't use it for marketing analytics, and we don't share it with third parties. Pro sync is stored encrypted — full details on the Security page.

local-first · minimal telemetry
02

We don't ask for bank access.

No OAuth to your bank, no third party on your transaction data, no "connect your account" step. You enter what you pay for — once, typically in a few minutes. In return, the accuracy stays yours.

no bank integration
03

Simple pricing, two plans.

Cancellable, exportable, portable.

FAQ

It really doesn't
touch my bank account?

Where does my data go?
On the Free plan, your subscription data is stored in your browser's localStorage — by default it's not sent to a server. We may use minimal, non-identifying technical data (e.g. anonymous page loads) for the service to function; details are in our Privacy Policy. On the Pro plan there's multi-device sync, stored encrypted; the full security model is documented on our Security page.
Why doesn't it connect to my bank automatically?
Because (1) bank integrations are heavy and geographically limited, (2) manual entry takes a few minutes once and gives you cleaner data than transaction-matching ever will, (3) many people don't want to hand banking tokens to a third party. We picked the privacy-first path — deliberately.
What if I clear my browser cache?
On Free, your data is gone — which is why we recommend exporting to CSV once a week (two export buttons under Settings). On Pro we sync it, so a new browser pulls it right back.
Where can I buy the Pro plan?
Purchases go through the Google Play Store — they handle billing and invoicing under their own terms. Your invoice will be available in your Google Play account after purchase.
What if you shut down the service?
You can export your data anytime in one click to CSV / JSON. Since Free runs in your browser, even after a shutdown you can still access your data through the export. A shutdown — if it ever happened — would be announced in advance, with transition time.
Why "leakd", by the way?
Because money often doesn't disappear in big chunks — it leaks: small monthly amounts that together can add up to a serious yearly bill. The name reminds you why you open the app.
